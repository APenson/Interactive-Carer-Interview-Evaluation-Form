Carer Interview Questions Guide

Building Confidence in Your Selection Process

Before the Interview

Preparation Checklist:

  • Review the carer's CV/application
  • Prepare your loved one's care summary
  • List specific needs and concerns
  • Plan for your loved one to meet the carer
  • Prepare the interview space
  • Allow 45-60 minutes for the interview

Documents to Request:

  • Valid DBS certificate
  • Proof of right to work in UK
  • Professional references
  • Training certificates
  • Insurance documentation
  • Previous employer references

Core Interview Questions

Experience & Qualifications

1. Tell us about your experience in dementia care.

Look for: Specific examples, understanding of condition, length of experience

2. What training have you completed in dementia care?

Look for: Formal qualifications, ongoing training, specialist courses

3. How many clients with dementia have you cared for?

Look for: Range of experience, different stages of dementia

4. What was your longest assignment and why did it end?

Look for: Consistency, professional approach to endings

5. Describe a challenging situation you've faced and how you handled it.

Look for: Problem-solving skills, patience, professional boundaries

Understanding of Dementia

6. How would you explain dementia to someone who doesn't understand it?

Look for: Clear communication, empathy, accurate knowledge

7. What are the different stages of dementia and how do care needs change?

Look for: Understanding of progression, adaptability

8. How do you approach someone who is confused or agitated?

Look for: De-escalation techniques, patience, person-centred approach

9. What would you do if someone refuses personal care?

Look for: Respect for dignity, alternative approaches, communication skills

10. How do you maintain someone's independence while keeping them safe?

Look for: Balance of support and autonomy, creative solutions

Personal Care Skills

11. Are you comfortable with all aspects of personal care?

Look for: Confidence, professionalism, specific experience

12. How do you approach intimate care while maintaining dignity?

Look for: Respect, communication, proper techniques

13. What would you do if you noticed changes in someone's health?

Look for: Observation skills, communication with family/professionals

14. How do you encourage someone to take medications they don't want?

Look for: Patience, understanding, appropriate techniques

15. Describe your approach to helping with mobility and transfers.

Look for: Safety awareness, proper techniques, confidence

Communication & Relationships

16. How do you build rapport with someone who has dementia?

Look for: Patience, understanding, person-centred approach

17. What do you do when someone doesn't remember who you are?

Look for: Flexibility, non-confrontational approach, patience

18. How do you communicate with family members about care?

Look for: Regular updates, honesty, professional boundaries

19. Tell us about a time you had to deal with a difficult family situation.

Look for: Professionalism, empathy, conflict resolution

20. How do you handle confidentiality?

Look for: Understanding of boundaries, discretion, professionalism

Practical Scenarios

21. What would you do if [loved one's name] became distressed during personal care?

Look for: Specific strategies, patience, flexibility

22. How would you respond if they wanted to go home (when they are at home)?

Look for: Understanding of confusion, redirection techniques

23. What if they refuse to eat or drink?

Look for: Encouragement strategies, when to seek help

24. How would you handle a situation where they want to leave the house alone?

Look for: Safety awareness, distraction techniques, appropriate responses

25. What would you do in a medical emergency?

Look for: First aid knowledge, emergency procedures, staying calm

Personality & Approach Questions

Values & Attitude

26. What do you find most rewarding about dementia care?

Look for: Genuine care, positive attitude, understanding of value

27. How do you cope with the emotional aspects of dementia care?

Look for: Emotional resilience, healthy coping mechanisms

28. What does person-centred care mean to you?

Look for: Understanding of individual needs, respect for preferences

29. How do you maintain your own wellbeing while caring for others?

Look for: Self-care awareness, professional boundaries

30. What would you do if you made a mistake?

Look for: Honesty, accountability, learning from errors

Practical Considerations

31. Are you comfortable with household tasks as part of care?

Look for: Willingness, understanding of holistic care

32. How do you feel about pets in the home?

Look for: Comfort level, any allergies or concerns

33. What are your views on maintaining routines?

Look for: Understanding of importance, flexibility when needed

34. How do you approach activities and stimulation?

Look for: Creativity, understanding of abilities, person-centred approach

35. What would you do if family members disagreed about care approaches?

Look for: Diplomacy, professional boundaries, communication skills

Specific Situation Questions - Tailored to Your Loved One

36. [Loved one's name] enjoys [specific activity]. How would you support this?

Look for: Interest in individual preferences, creative thinking

37. They sometimes [specific behaviour]. How would you respond?

Look for: Understanding, appropriate strategies, patience

38. Their mobility is [current state]. How would you support them safely?

Look for: Appropriate techniques, safety awareness

39. They have difficulty with [specific challenge]. What's your approach?

Look for: Understanding, practical solutions, patience

40. What questions do you have about [loved one's name] or their care needs?

Look for: Genuine interest, thoughtful questions, engagement

Availability & Practical Matters

Scheduling & Commitment

41. What are your available hours and days?

Look for: Match with your needs, flexibility, reliability

42. How much notice do you need for schedule changes?

Look for: Reasonable flexibility, understanding of care needs

43. What would you do if you were unwell and couldn't work?

Look for: Backup plans, responsibility, communication

44. Are you available for emergency situations?

Look for: Flexibility, understanding of care realities

45. How long are you hoping to stay in this position?

Look for: Commitment, stability, realistic expectations

Transport & Logistics

46. Do you drive and have your own transport?

Look for: Reliability, ability to support appointments

47. Are you comfortable with [specific location/area]?

Look for: Local knowledge, travel comfort

48. How would you handle appointments and outings?

Look for: Planning skills, confidence, experience

Questions for the Carer to Ask

Encourage Them to Ask:

49. What questions do you have about the role?

Look for: Genuine interest, thoughtful questions

50. Is there anything you'd like to know about [loved one's name]?

Look for: Person-centred interest, care planning

Good carers should ask about:

  • Daily routines and preferences
  • Specific care needs and challenges
  • Family involvement and communication
  • Emergency procedures and contacts
  • Training and development opportunities
  • Support from the agency or family

Red Flags and Positive Indicators

Red Flags to Watch For:

  • Vague or evasive answers
  • Lack of specific dementia knowledge
  • Unwillingness to discuss challenges
  • Poor communication skills
  • Inflexibility or rigidity
  • Lack of empathy or understanding
  • Inappropriate personal disclosures
  • Unrealistic promises
  • Reluctance to follow procedures
  • No questions about the person or role

Positive Indicators:

  • Specific examples and experiences
  • Clear understanding of dementia
  • Empathy and patience
  • Professional boundaries
  • Flexibility and adaptability
  • Good communication skills
  • Genuine interest in the person
  • Asks thoughtful questions
  • Realistic about challenges
  • Commitment to ongoing learning

After the Interview

Reference Check Questions:

  1. How long did they work for you?
  2. What were their main responsibilities?
  3. How did they handle challenging situations?
  4. Were they reliable and punctual?
  5. How did they communicate with family?
  6. Would you employ them again?
  7. Any areas for improvement?

Final Decision Factors:

  • Qualifications and experience match needs
  • Personality fits with loved one
  • Professional references check out
  • Availability matches requirements
  • Family feels comfortable and confident
  • Budget and terms agreed
  • All documentation verified

Remember: Trust your instincts. The right carer will make you feel confident and reassured about your loved one's care.

Use this guide to ensure thorough, consistent interviews that help you select the best possible carer for your loved one.
